Complete Guide in CyberSecurity

Cybersecurity is the practice of protecting systems, networks, applications, and data from unauthorized access, digital attacks, and disruption.

As more of the world’s infrastructure moves online—from banking and healthcare to communication and transportation—the importance of strong security practices grows every year.

Cybersecurity combines technology, processes, and people to detect threats, prevent attacks, and respond to incidents that could compromise data or operations.

Modern cybersecurity covers everything from network protection and identity management to defending against malware, phishing, and ransomware attacks. Governments, businesses, and individuals rely on cybersecurity tools and strategies to reduce risk and maintain trust in digital systems.

What Cybersecurity Is and Why It Exists

Cybersecurity refers to the set of technologies, practices, and policies designed to protect digital systems, networks, and data from unauthorized access and malicious attacks. Every device connected to the internet creates potential security risks. Cybersecurity aims to prevent these risks from turning into breaches that could expose sensitive data or disrupt services.
