Cybersecurity is fundamentally about risk management in the digital environment. Computers, servers, mobile devices, and cloud services constantly exchange data across networks. Each connection creates a potential vulnerability that attackers could exploit.

According to research and definitions widely used by organizations such as IBM and Cisco, cybersecurity protects three core elements:

  1. People – users, employees, administrators, and customers who interact with systems.
  2. Processes – rules, policies, and procedures that determine how systems are secured.
  3. Technology – tools such as firewalls, encryption systems, and intrusion detection systems.

Cybersecurity therefore is not just a technical discipline. It is also about organizational strategy and human behavior.

The rise of cloud computing, remote work, and artificial intelligence has expanded the digital attack surface dramatically. Attackers now target everything from corporate databases to personal smartphones.

Without strong cybersecurity, attackers can steal confidential data, disrupt critical infrastructure, or manipulate systems for financial or political gain.
