Types of Cyber Security

Cyber threats come in many forms, and they continue to evolve as technology changes.

Some of the most common cybersecurity threats include:

Malware

Malware is malicious software designed to infiltrate or damage systems. It includes viruses, worms, trojan programs, and spyware.

Ransomware

Ransomware encrypts files or entire systems and demands payment to restore access. This type of attack has become one of the most financially damaging cyber threats.

Phishing

Phishing attacks trick users into revealing sensitive information such as passwords or financial data. Attackers often use fake emails or websites that imitate trusted organizations.

Distributed Denial of Service Attack

A distributed denial of service attack overwhelms servers or networks with traffic so legitimate users cannot access them.

Social Engineering

Social engineering manipulates people psychologically rather than technically. Attackers convince victims to disclose information or grant access.

Identity and Credential Theft

Attackers steal login credentials to access systems and move laterally across networks.

The cybersecurity landscape evolves rapidly because attackers constantly develop new techniques to bypass security systems.
