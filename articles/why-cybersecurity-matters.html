What Cyber Security is and Why it Exists

Cybersecurity is the safety of digital objects like phones and computers.

⏱ 2 min read • Cybersecurity

Why Cyber Security is Critical Today

The importance of cybersecurity has increased dramatically over the last two decades. Businesses, governments, and individuals store enormous amounts of sensitive data online.

This data includes:

  1. financial records
  2. medical information
  3. intellectual property
  4. government intelligence
  5. personal communications

When attackers compromise these systems, the consequences can be severe.

Major cyber incidents can cause:

  1. Financial damage from fraud or ransomware payments
  2. Operational disruption that stops businesses or services
  3. Reputation damage that reduces customer trust
  4. National security risks when critical infrastructure is targeted

The economic cost of cybercrime is estimated in the trillions of dollars globally each year, according to cybersecurity industry reports and government research.

Cybersecurity also protects critical infrastructure systems including:

  1. power grids
  2. transportation networks
  3. healthcare systems
  4. financial markets

Because these systems rely on interconnected networks, even a small vulnerability can cascade into large-scale disruption.
